## The documentary foundations (1947–1953)

The oldest material is not a set of events but a set of **institutional documents**: the [[Source - DOW-UAP-D084 US Army Flying Saucer Study 1949|1949 Army Flying Saucer Study]], the [[Source - DOW-UAP-D086 US Navy Flying Discs Report 1948|1948 Navy Flying Discs report]], the [[Source - DOW-UAP-D087-D088 USAF Analysis of Flying Objects|USAF Analysis of Flying Objects]], the [[Source - 1952 Samford Memorandum Flying Saucers|1952 Samford memorandum]], and the [[Source - CIA-UAP-002 Robertson Panel Report|1953 CIA Robertson Panel report]] (which set the Air Force Blue Book debunking posture for a decade).

These anchor [[Thesis - Historical Continuity of Air Force Position 1952 to 2026]] (*provisionally supported, high*): the U.S. Air Force institutional position has been **remarkably stable since 1952** — phenomena are real, observations are credible, attribution to foreign or extraterrestrial origin is unsupported, and the response is sustained institutional attention without resolution. The 2025–2026 AARO-era language rhymes with the 1952 language. See the [[Historical Continuity 1952 to 2026.canvas|continuity Canvas]] and the [[Timeline 1944-2026]].

## Isolated cases

A few events sit outside every regional cluster — typically a single cable or report from one location:

- [[Incident - 2008-07-02 Harare Zimbabwe Disc|Harare, Zimbabwe (2008)]] — a SECRET//NOFORN CIA cable describing a disc over Harare airport
- [[Incident - 2025 Djibouti D8 Two White-Hot UAPs|Djibouti (2025)]] — two white-hot objects, AFRICOM
- [[Incident - 1996-09 D48|1996 analytic study (D48)]] and [[Incident - 2000-02 D49 Launch Summary|2000 launch summary (D49)]] — older catalog/analytic items, both *assessed-conventional*

(The 1968 [[Incident - 1968-03 Himalayan Border UAP Wave|Himalayan border wave]] is covered under [[Brief - INDOPACOM and the Pacific]].)

## A methodological note

Not every "UAP"-tagged document is substantively about UAP.
