> [!SUMMARY]
> After the 2020 maritime cluster, CENTCOM reporting continues as a steady stream of mission-report (MISREP) and range-fouler observations across [[Location - Iraq]], [[Location - Syria]], the [[Location - United Arab Emirates]], and the Gulf through 2022–2024.

## What it is

These are individual UAP observations embedded in routine air operations over Iraq and Syria (largely under [[Operation Inherent Resolve]]) and the wider Gulf. Most are brief sensor-and-visual contacts logged in standardized reports, frequently with redacted platform/unit fields. The corridor is heavily trafficked by ISR balloons, surveillance aerostats, drones, and Russian aircraft, which complicates clean attribution — many remain **unresolved**, a few are assessed **probable-conventional**.

## The standout case

The best-documented is the [[Incident - 2023-02-21 Shaddadi F-15E|2023 Shaddadi F-15E event]]: a two-ship received three minutes of radar jamming on the [[Sensor - APG-82 Radar|APG-82]] (assessed as a Turkish ground-based X-band emitter, *not* UAP-caused), and **one minute later** observed three IR-significant white objects at FL240 with no radar return. It is a clean instance of two of the collection's recurring patterns at once — [[Thesis - Active EW Co-occurrence with UAP Observations|EW co-occurrence]] and [[Thesis - High-Altitude Maneuvering Observations Have Unusual Evidentiary Weight|high-altitude observation]] — with the EW given an explicit conventional attribution while the objects themselves stay unresolved.

## The frame

This stream is the clearest illustration of the [[Thesis - CENTCOM Sensor Density Hypothesis|sensor-density thesis]] (*high confidence*): CENTCOM dominates the corpus because that is where U.S. sensors and reporting infrastructure are concentrated, not necessarily where unusual objects concentrate. See the [[CENTCOM Reporting Infrastructure.canvas|reporting-infrastructure Canvas]] for how a MISREP/range-fouler reaches AARO.
