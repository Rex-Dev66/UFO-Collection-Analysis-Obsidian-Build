## The strongest patterns (high-confidence)

- **Geography is a sensor artifact.** [[Thesis - CENTCOM Sensor Density Hypothesis|Reporting density follows U.S. sensors and missions]], not object density. CENTCOM's ~70% share reflects where the U.S. has eyes and a reporting pipeline — a lens to apply to every "hot spot" in the data.
- **Most "famous" material resolves conventionally.** The [[Thesis - Apollo Debris Misidentification Pattern|Apollo observations]] are largely debris/artifacts identified in real time, and many [[Thesis - Keyword Sweep False Positives|keyword-swept documents]] aren't substantively about UAP at all.
- **Testimony is fragile.** [[Thesis - Eyewitness Divergence in Transient Aerial Events|Multiple credible witnesses routinely diverge]] on size/shape/behavior for the same event — no exotic object property required to explain it.
- **The institutional posture is stable.** [[Thesis - Historical Continuity of Air Force Position 1952 to 2026|The Air Force position has barely moved since 1952]]: phenomena real, observations credible, attribution unsupported, attention sustained without resolution.

## Resolved vs unresolved

Of ~53 incidents, the large majority are logged **unresolved**, with a handful **assessed-conventional** (e.g. [[Incident - 2016-11-18 Latakia P-8A|Latakia/KCTG]], the conventional [[Incident - 1972 Apollo 17 Fragment Field|Apollo debris]] cases), a few **probable-conventional**, and a few **inconclusive** (e.g. [[Incident - 2022-02-15 Colorado Springs Cheyenne Mountain|Colorado Springs]], the [[Incident - 2025-04-10 INDOPACOM Tearline|INDOPACOM tearlines]]). The key caveat: **"unresolved" is not "exotic."** It mostly means *insufficient data to attribute* in environments cluttered with drones, balloons, aerostats, and adversary aircraft — not evidence of anything beyond the conventional. (Live breakdown: [[MOC - Incidents by Year]].)

## The genuinely weighty cases

Three things resist easy conventional dismissal and deserve the most scrutiny:

1. [[Thesis - High-Altitude Maneuvering Observations Have Unusual Evidentiary Weight|High-altitude maneuvering]] — objects maneuvering above the service ceiling of known aircraft (anchor: [[Incident - 1994-01-27 Kazakhstan 747SP|Kazakhstan 1994]]).
2. [[Thesis - Active EW Co-occurrence with UAP Observations|EW co-occurrence]] — electronic interference on the same platform/sortie as the observation ([[Incident - 2020-10 D58 Range Fouler|D58]], [[Incident - 2023-02-21 Shaddadi F-15E|Shaddadi]]).
3. Multi-object **formations** that hold station with no thermal-exhaust signature (the [[Brief - 2020 Arabian Gulf Cluster|2020 Gulf cluster]]).

## Open questions / leads

- **The Rhodes photographs** — Captain Ed Rhodes reportedly photographed the [[Incident - 1994-01-27 Kazakhstan 747SP|1994 Kazakhstan]] object; locating them is the highest-value single lead.
- **Frame correlation** — are the [[FBI Photo Series Index|FBI Photo stills]] frames from the DOD video corpus? Matching timestamps would tie stills to incidents.
- **Apollo 12 gap** — a [[Witness - Apollo 12 Crew|witness note]] and transcript exist with no incident note; an Apollo 12 incident may be missing.
- **Is EW co-occurrence causal or coincidental?** The collection does not yet decide ([[Thesis - Active EW Co-occurrence with UAP Observations|see thesis]]).
- **Instrument-grade domestic data** — the [[Thesis - Federal Law Enforcement as a UAP Reporting Channel|federal-agent channel]] is still testimony-dominated; no sensor-grade data yet.
