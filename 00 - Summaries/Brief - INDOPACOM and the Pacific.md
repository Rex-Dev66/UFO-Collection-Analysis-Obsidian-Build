## What it is

The modern Indo-Pacific entries are sparse, redacted tearline summaries — the [[Incident - 2025-04-10 INDOPACOM Tearline|April 2025 INDOPACOM tearlines]], a [[Incident - 2023 Japan D42 Range Fouler|2023 Japan range-fouler]], and a [[Incident - 2024 East China Sea D28|2024 East China Sea]] event — most of which are *inconclusive* or *unresolved* on the available text. The deeper value of this group is its **historical** members.

## The standout case

The [[Incident - 1994-01-27 Kazakhstan 747SP|1994 Kazakhstan 747SP observation]] is among the most evidentially weighty in the entire collection. Tajik Air chief pilot [[Witness - Captain Ed Rhodes and Tajik Air Crew|Captain Ed Rhodes]] (an American, with two former-PanAm colleagues) watched an object for ~40 minutes from FL410, maneuvering in "circles, corkscrews and 90-degree turns," with contrails he estimated at ~100,000 ft. It is the anchor case for [[Thesis - High-Altitude Maneuvering Observations Have Unusual Evidentiary Weight|the high-altitude-maneuvering thesis]] (*open, moderate*): objects maneuvering well above the service ceiling of known manned aircraft carry unusual weight because conventional explanations (balloons, satellites, debris) don't maneuver that way. **Rhodes reportedly photographed it with a pocket Olympus** — recovering those photos is one of the collection's highest-value open leads.
