> [!SUMMARY]
> The newest line of evidence in the collection: domestic (NORTHCOM-AOR) UAP cases reported by U.S. service members, **federal agents**, and civilians between 2021 and 2026 — a structural shift away from the overseas-military center of gravity.

## What it is

The PURSUE Release 03 tranche introduced the collection's first large body of **domestic** cases over the U.S. homeland. They differ in character from the CENTCOM corpus: more **testimony-dominated**, with trained ground observers (federal agents, soldiers) and civilians rather than airborne sensor pods. The marquee events are the [[Incident - 2023-10 Western US Event Orbs Launching Orbs|Western U.S. "Event"]] (a luminous orange "mother orb" repeatedly producing smaller red orbs near a sensitive site, witnessed by six federal special agents), the [[Incident - 2022-02-15 Colorado Springs Cheyenne Mountain|2022 Colorado Springs / Cheyenne Mountain]] object, and a run of [[Incident - 2024 Northeastern US Plasma Orbs|Northeastern]] [[Incident - 2026 Northeastern US Red Orb Sighting|orb sightings]].

## What it shows

Two patterns are doing the work here. First, [[Thesis - Domestic CONUS UAP Reporting Emerges 2021-2026|the domestic-emergence thesis]] (*provisionally supported, medium*): the corpus's center of gravity is moving home. Second, [[Thesis - Federal Law Enforcement as a UAP Reporting Channel|the FBI/federal-agent channel]] (*medium*): U.S. law enforcement now functions as a distinct, credible reporting and investigative pipeline parallel to the military MISREP system, feeding AARO with trained-observer ground reporting (e.g. the Colorado Springs FD-302 and the [[Source - ICA-UAP-D001 Colorado Springs Analysis|ICA analysis]]). Because so many of these rest on eyewitness accounts, [[Thesis - Eyewitness Divergence in Transient Aerial Events|eyewitness-divergence]] (*high*) is the key caution: multiple credible witnesses to the same transient event routinely diverge on size, shape, and behavior — which does not require any unusual property of the object.
